Ethopian Boeing 737-MAX 8 Crash

New Ethopian

Investigators trying to find the cause of the crash of Ethopian Fight 302, has located the aircraft's flight data recorder.

Investigators working on the ill-fated Ethopian flight 302, has located the flight data recorder of the aircraft. Information from the recorder has not been released to the public, it is assumed that investigators are still working on the information made available.

Ethopian 302, was a scheduled passenger flight from the Ethopian capital, Addis Ababa, to the Kenyan capital, Nairobi. The aircraft crashed six minutes after take-off, near the town of Bishoftu, this resulted in the deaths of all 157 passengers and crew.

Remaining parts of the crashed 737 MAX 8.

The aircraft, a Boeing 737 MAX 8, registered as ET-AVJ, was delievered to Ethopian on the 15th of November, making it around four months old at the time of the incident. Boeing stated in a press release that;

"Boeing is deeply saddened to learn of the passing of the passengers and crew on Ethiopian Airlines Flight 302, a 737 MAX 8 airplane. We extend our heartfelt sympathies to the families and loved ones of the passengers and crew on board and stand ready to support the Ethiopian Airlines team. A Boeing technical team will be travelling to the crash site to provide technical assistance under the direction of the Ethiopia Accident Investigation Bureau and U.S. National Transportation Safety Board."

It is known that the pilot of the aircraft has been flying for the airline since 2010 and is a "senior pilot" with more than 8,000 flight hours and an "excellent record," according to Gebre Mariam.

Mutiple governments around the world have prohibited the 737 MAX from flying within their airspace, most noticably the EASA and the CAA.

**Update 14/03/2019**

The FAA has prohibited the Boeing 737 MAX Family from operating within American airspace, this is after President Trump stated that "airliners are becoming too complex" and after vowing to "ban" the aircraft till the issues are fixed.
